# Idle Raiders - Complete Game Lore Bible

## Overview

**Idle Raiders** is a fantasy idle RPG set in the **Five Realms**, a world ravaged by war, ancient evils, and monstrous threats. Players command heroes, collect powerful cards, and lead their guilds through dungeons, boss raids, and territory conquest.

---

## The World: The Five Realms

The Five Realms is a vast continent divided into five major territories, each with its own unique environment, threats, and history. The realms were once united under a single ruler who wore the **Crown of Dominion**, but that age of peace ended when darkness spread across the land.

### Territory 1: Evershade

**Level Range:** 1-28  
**Environment:** Ancient mystical forests

> "Deep within ancient woods lies Evershade, a realm hidden beneath towering canopies where sunlight struggles to reach the forest floor. For centuries it stood untouched by war. Now the forest trembles."

Evershade is the starting realm, a land of dense forests and elven settlements. The ancient woods have long been a sanctuary, but the balance has been disturbed. Goblin tribes have united under a new warlord, and the Spider Queen's brood threatens to consume everything.

**Key Locations:**
- **Goblin Cave** - A network of tunnels where goblin smiths forge crude weapons
- **Spider Den** - A vast cavern of webs where the Broodmother reigns

**Major Threats:**
- **Goblin King** - A warlord who united the scattered goblin tribes into an organized army
- **Spider Queen** - A colossal brood mother feeding her swarm with captured travelers

**Notable Materials:**
- Goblin Iron - Crude metal smelted by goblin smiths
- Crude Leather - Hide from goblin beasts
- Silk Thread - Harvested from spider webs
- Chitin Plate - Shell fragments from giant spiders

### Territory 2: Sunspire Citadel

**Level Range:** 31-58  
**Environment:** Golden walls and sacred plains

> "South of the forest, the golden towers of Sunspire watch over the sacred plains. Beneath those plains lie ancient battlefields where thousands of warriors were buried. Something has begun waking them."

Sunspire Citadel is a bastion of light and holy power, defended by paladins and battle mages. But the sacred plains hide a terrible secret - the Crypt of the Undying, where a necromancer king has returned to raise armies of the dead.

**Key Locations:**
- **Graveyard of Souls** - Ancient battlefields where spirits wander and skeletons rise
- **Crypt of the Undying** - A tomb where the Lich King rules over undead legions

**Major Threats:**
- **Soul Reaver** - A wraith that feeds on wandering spirits, growing stronger with each soul
- **Lich King** - A necromancer king whose power is bound to a hidden phylactery

**Notable Materials:**
- Soul Ash - Residue left by restless spirits
- Grave Cloth - Decayed fabric from burial wraps
- Necro Dust - Powdered bone infused with death magic
- Bone Rune - Carved bones etched with arcane symbols

### Territory 3: Frosthold

**Level Range:** 61-88  
**Environment:** Frozen wastes and eternal winter

> "Frosthold stands amid glaciers and frozen cliffs where the wind never stops howling. Yet even the eternal winter is changing."

Frosthold is a land of perpetual ice where only the hardiest survive. Rangers track beasts across glaciers, and hunters brave the frozen wilderness. But ancient forces are stirring - a Frost Giant commands the blizzards, and the Dark Forest's trees have begun to twist and move.

**Key Locations:**
- **Ice Cavern** - Frozen tunnels where the Frost Giant sleeps
- **Dark Forest** - A corrupted woodland where trees move and roots lash out

**Major Threats:**
- **Frost Giant** - A towering titan who commands blizzards
- **Ancient Treant** - A colossal forest guardian corrupted by dark magic

**Notable Materials:**
- Frostwood - Petrified wood preserved by ancient ice
- Glacial Shard - Magical ice that never melts
- Elder Bark - Bark from sentient trees
- Living Sap - Forest sap imbued with life force

### Territory 4: Ember City

**Level Range:** 91-118  
**Environment:** Volcanic hellscape and molten forges

> "Ember City is built upon rivers of lava where blacksmiths forge the finest weapons in the realm. But now the flames have turned hostile."

Ember City is the industrial heart of the Five Realms, where volcanic forges produce legendary weapons and armor. The city is built around active lava flows, and its workers are hardened by the heat. But demons have emerged from portals of fire, and the Ash Lord commands a fiery legion from the Ashen Fortress.

**Key Locations:**
- **Molten Quarry** - Volcanic mines where fire creatures now roam
- **Ashen Fortress** - A ruined stronghold burning with unnatural flames

**Major Threats:**
- **Ember Colossus** - A molten giant that rises from the lava
- **Ash Lord** - A demon commander who rules the fiery legion

**Notable Materials:**
- Cinder Stone - Volcanic rock still warm to the touch
- Magma Core - Crystallized heart of molten rock
- Ash Crystal - Rare crystals formed in volcanic ash
- Charred Bone - Bones hardened and blackened by intense heat

### Territory 5: Iron Citadel

**Level Range:** 121-150  
**Environment:** Impenetrable fortress and seat of power

> "The final defense of the world stands at the Iron Citadel. Beyond its gates lies the abyss."

The Iron Citadel is humanity's last bastion against the ultimate evil. Its walls have never fallen, and its champions are the greatest warriors in the realm. But a tear in reality has opened near the citadel, and demons pour through the gate. Beneath a mountain of gold, the Ancient Dragon - oldest of its kind - has awakened.

**Key Locations:**
- **Demon's Gate** - A rift in reality where demons invade
- **Dragon's Lair** - A vast cavern filled with the hoard of forgotten empires

**Major Threats:**
- **Demon Lord** - Conqueror of countless worlds, leading the invasion
- **Ancient Dragon** - The oldest and most powerful dragon, the ultimate threat

**Notable Materials:**
- Demon Ichor - Foul fluid from demonic veins
- Cursed Steel - Metal corrupted by demonic energy
- Dragon Bone - Dense bone fragments from dragon skeletons
- Void Scale - Scales crackling with void energy

## Guild System

Guilds are the backbone of organized resistance against the threats of the Five Realms. Players can join guilds to:

- **Raid Bosses** - Coordinate attacks against world bosses
- **Guild Wars** - Compete for territory control and guild glory
- **Share Resources** - Donate materials and support fellow members

### Guild Wars

Weekly guild wars pit guilds against each other for control of strategic outposts and enemy strongholds. Guilds earn war points through:

- Capturing neutral outposts
- Attacking rival guild strongholds
- Dealing damage in war missions

The victorious guilds earn glory and valuable rewards at the end of each war season.

## The Phoenix Merchant Guild

### Origins

The Phoenix Merchant Guild is one of the oldest and most influential trading organizations in the Five Realms. Founded during the Age of Unity, when the Crown of Dominion still kept peace across the lands, the guild was established by a consortium of wealthy merchants who sought to create safe trade routes between all five territories.

The guild takes its name from the legendary Storm Phoenix - the mythical bird that rides thunderstorm winds above the Iron Citadel. The founders believed that just as the phoenix rises from ashes, trade and commerce would rise again after every war and disaster. Their motto: **"From Ashes, Prosperity."**

### History

During the golden age, the Phoenix Merchant Guild operated caravans across all territories, their distinctive crimson and gold banners guaranteeing safe passage even through the most dangerous regions. They maintained trading posts in every major city:

- **Evershade Branch** - Dealing in rare silks, forest herbs, and elven crafts
- **Sunspire Branch** - Trading in blessed weapons, holy relics, and fine cloth
- **Frosthold Branch** - Specializing in furs, glacial crystals, and preserved meats
- **Ember City Branch** - Controlling the trade of forged weapons and volcanic materials
- **Iron Citadel Branch** - The headquarters, handling the most valuable transactions

When the wars began and darkness spread across the realms, many trade routes became too dangerous. The guild adapted by establishing fortified trading posts and hiring mercenary escorts. They also expanded into new ventures - including entertainment and hospitality - to maintain income during the turbulent times.

### Structure

The Phoenix Merchant Guild is led by the **Grand Phoenix** - a title passed down through election by the senior guild masters. Below the Grand Phoenix are:

- **Phoenix Masters** - Regional leaders who control each territory's operations
- **Phoenix Keepers** - Senior merchants who manage major establishments
- **Phoenix Traders** - Journeyman merchants who handle day-to-day trade
- **Phoenix Apprentices** - New members learning the trade

The guild's colors are **crimson red** and **burnished gold**, representing fire and wealth. Their emblem is a stylized phoenix rising from flames, clutching a merchant's scale in its talons.

### Philosophy

The Phoenix Merchant Guild maintains strict neutrality in political conflicts. They trade with all factions - even selling supplies to opposing sides of guild wars (though they take care never to provide weapons that could be traced back to them). Their philosophy is simple: **"Gold knows no allegiance."**

However, they draw firm lines against trading with demons or undead forces. The guild considers such creatures threats to civilization itself - and more importantly, dead men make poor customers.

### Current Operations

With the realm in constant conflict, the Phoenix Merchant Guild has diversified its operations:

1. **Trade Caravans** - Still operating between safer routes
2. **Banking Services** - Secure storage and loans for adventurers
3. **Information Brokering** - Selling maps, intelligence, and secrets
4. **Entertainment Venues** - Taverns, gambling halls, and rest houses
5. **Mercenary Contracts** - Connecting adventurers with paying clients

Their most famous entertainment venture is **The Ember Tavern** - a legendary establishment that has become synonymous with both pleasure and profit.

## The Ember Tavern

### Location

The Ember Tavern sits at the crossroads of Ember City's merchant district, built atop an ancient lava tube that provides natural heating throughout the building. The location was chosen specifically for its position at the intersection of three major trade routes:

- The **Iron Road** leading to the Iron Citadel
- The **Frost Trail** connecting to Frosthold
- The **Pilgrim's Path** traveling to Sunspire

This strategic position ensures a constant flow of travelers, merchants, adventurers, and soldiers - all potential customers.

### Architecture

The Ember Tavern is a marvel of Ember City engineering. The main building is constructed from **black basalt** quarried from the Molten Quarry, with decorative elements of **volcanic glass** and **brass fittings**. The signature feature is the **Eternal Hearth** - a massive fireplace at the center of the main hall that burns with flames channeled directly from the lava tube below. These flames burn with an unusual orange-crimson hue and never need fuel.

The building has three main levels:

**Ground Floor - The Common Hall**
- The main tavern area with the Eternal Hearth
- A full bar serving drinks from across the Five Realms
- Stage for entertainers and announcements
- General gambling tables for casual games

**Second Floor - The Phoenix Nest**
- Private dining rooms for wealthy patrons
- High-stakes gaming rooms
- Meeting chambers for business negotiations
- The famous **Ember Baths** - hot spring pools heated by volcanic vents

**Third Floor - The Crimson Wing**
- VIP gambling suites
- The **Dragon's Hoard** room for the highest-stakes games
- Private quarters for the most important guests
- The Phoenix Keeper's office and guild administration

**Basement Level - The Lava Lounge**
- Underground entertainment venue
- Arena for pit fighting and exotic creature matches
- Vault storage for the guild's Ember City treasury
- Secret escape tunnels (for emergencies)

### Atmosphere

The Ember Tavern is designed to feel warm, luxurious, and slightly dangerous. The perpetual orange glow from the Eternal Hearth casts dancing shadows across the walls, while the warmth provides comfort to travelers from colder regions. The air carries hints of exotic spices, pipe smoke, and the faint sulfurous tang of the volcanic vents.

**Decor includes:**
- Tapestries depicting the Phoenix Merchant Guild's history
- Trophies from famous hunts and battles (donated by patrons)
- Caged fire sprites that provide ambient lighting
- Volcanic glass sculptures of phoenixes and dragons
- Maps of the Five Realms marking guild trade routes

### Services

**Drinks and Dining:**
- **Dragonfire Ale** - A potent brew with a chili kick (specialty)
- **Frosthold Mead** - Sweet honey wine from the frozen north
- **Sunspire Wine** - Blessed vintage from sacred vineyards
- **Evershade Tea** - Calming forest herb blends
- **Demon's Blood** - A dangerously strong spirit (not actual demon blood... probably)

**Entertainment:**
- Live music from traveling bards
- Fire dancers and acrobatic performers
- Fortune tellers and mystic readings
- Storytellers sharing tales of adventure

**Gambling:**
- Dice games of chance
- Card games requiring skill and bluffing
- Wheel of fortune spins
- Betting on arena fights
- High-stakes poker in private rooms

**Other Services:**
- Room rentals for overnight stays
- Hot bath access
- Message relay services
- Discrete meetings and deal-making
- Information for sale (at a premium)

### Staff

The Ember Tavern employs a diverse staff:

- **Phoenix Keeper Varrus** - The establishment's manager, a former adventurer who lost his leg fighting demons. His prosthetic is made of enchanted volcanic glass.
- **Bartenders** - Skilled mixologists who know everyone's secrets
- **Servers** - Quick and attentive, trained to spot trouble
- **Dealers** - Professional gaming operators bound by guild honor codes
- **Bouncers** - Retired mercenaries who maintain order (usually former adventurers)
- **Entertainers** - Singers, dancers, and performers from across the realms

### House Rules

The Ember Tavern operates under strict rules:

1. **No weapons drawn** - Weapons must remain sheathed. Violators are banned.
2. **Debts are honored** - The guild has a long memory and longer reach.
3. **No magic at the tables** - Cheating with magic results in permanent ban and public shaming.
4. **What happens in the Ember stays in the Ember** - Discretion is guaranteed for all patrons.
5. **The House always pays** - Winners receive their earnings promptly and in full.
6. **Guild neutrality** - No guild war disputes inside the establishment.

### Reputation

The Ember Tavern is known throughout the Five Realms as:

- A place where deals are made and fortunes change hands
- A neutral ground where enemies can drink side by side
- A source of reliable information (for the right price)
- The finest entertainment venue in Ember City
- A dangerous place to cross the house

Many legendary tales begin or end at the Ember Tavern. Heroes celebrate victories here. Rogues plan heists over drinks. Merchants seal contracts worth kingdoms. And more than a few adventurers have gambled away their life savings chasing one more win.

### Famous Patrons

The Ember Tavern has hosted many notable figures:

- **Aurelion the Dragonlord** - Supposedly celebrated his victory over the Ancient Dragon here
- **Nyx Shadowblade** - Uses the tavern as a neutral meeting point for contracts
- **The Five Lords** - Guild war leaders have negotiated ceasefires at its tables
- **Countless adventurers** - Anyone who's anyone in the Five Realms has passed through

### The Ember Tavern Saying

Among regulars, there's a common toast:

> **"May your drinks be strong, your dice be lucky, and your debts be paid - for the Phoenix remembers."**
